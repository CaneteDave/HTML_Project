VANS OLD SKOOL

The Old Skool was the first sneaker from
Vans to feature the now-iconic wavy side
stripe, or the “Jazz Stripe,” as it’s officially
called by the brand. The Old Skool debuted in 1977,
and was originally named the “Style 36.” It was
also the first Vans model to incorporate leather/suede
paneling, which increased durability in comparison to
the all-canvas styles from the brand. Utilized only as
a skate shoe for core skateboarders at first, the Old
Skool eventually found its way onto the feet on
non-skaters strictly for casual use, and today
the timeless design is more popular than ever.
